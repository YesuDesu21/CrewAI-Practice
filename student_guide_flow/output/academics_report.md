**Strategies for Balancing Leadership Duties and Heavy Core Coursework**

To maintain an exceptional semester grade profile while managing extensive coursework, organizational commitments, and leadership duties, employ the following strategies:

1.  Regular Progress Checks:
    *   Set aside dedicated time each week to review task completion and progress toward set goals.
    *   Schedule time for self-reflection and adjustments as needed.

2.  Prioritize Deep Work Sessions:
    *   Allocate specific blocks of uninterrupted deep-work sessions for core coursework, particularly in bottleneck courses like ENGG 4100 (Fluid Mechanics).
    *   Use the Pomodoro Technique to optimize focus during these sessions.

3.  Leverage Multitasking Strategically:
    *   Allocate dedicated blocks of time for group project coordination and team meetings.
    *   Balance multitasking with regular breaks and self-care activities to avoid burnout.

4.  Utilize Breaks Wisely (Leisure Time):
    *   Use scheduled break times for relaxation, self-improvement, or meeting up with friends rather than procrastination.

5.  Set Boundaries:
    *   Maintain clear communication boundaries between personal life and university commitments.
    *   Establish routines that support work-life balance to prevent burnout.

**Implementation and Continuous Evaluation:**

Regularly review the weekly schedule with the student to ensure it remains aligned with their goals, progress, and areas of improvement. Encourage open communication to adjust the plan as needed and maintain alignment with performance analyst recommendations for success on the Deanís List.
